HERITAGE

Chasewater reservoir was first created in 1797 as a canal feeder and to transport colliery produce. It remains the largest canal feeder reservoir in use today. In June 1799, the dam burst, sending millions of gallons of water down the valley, causing devastating local damage. By March 1800, the dam was rebuilt, and the reservoir refilled. In 2006, a routine dam inspection revealed that remedial works were needed to prevent a re-run of 1799! Restoration work began in 2010 and competed in 2012.
